About theory.so

My name is David E. Wheeler, and this is my database blog. Why .so? Think of it as standing for “set operations”. Or maybe “stored objects”. Hey, there is no .db domain, so you do what you can, am I right?

I’ve been hacking databases since 1997, when I started messing around with FileMaker Pro and Microsoft Access. Since 2001, I’ve focused primarily on [PostgreSQL], for which I have developed PGXN, a service for distributing extensions, and pgTAP, a unit testing framework. More recently, I’ve been working on Sqitch, a database schema change management app for PostgreSQL, Oracle, SQLite, and MySQL.

I’m @theory on Twitter and also @theory on App.Net.

You can hire me to hack PostgreSQL and Perl via my consulting firm, PostgreSQL Experts.
